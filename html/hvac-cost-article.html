How Much Does It Cost to Replace an HVAC System in Las Vegas?

If you live in Las Vegas, you know how important it is to have a reliable and efficient HVAC system to keep you comfortable all year round. But what if your old system breaks down or needs frequent repairs? How much will it cost you to replace it with a new one?

The answer depends on several factors, such as the size and type of your HVAC system, the size and layout of your home, the condition and length of your ductwork, and the brand and efficiency rating of the new unit. The good news is that replacing your HVAC system in Las Vegas, NV can be affordable and easy with the right company.

In this article, we will break down these factors and give you some ballpark figures to help you budget for your HVAC replacement project for your Las Vegas home.

Factor #1: The Size and Type of Your HVAC System

The cost of replacing an HVAC system in Las Vegas, NV depends on several factors, such as the size and efficiency of the unit, the complexity of the installation, and the quality of the service. According to 5 Estimates1, the average cost to replace a central A/C unit in Las Vegas, NV for a 1500 – 3000 square foot home is between $4,475 – $14,190.

The first factor that affects the cost of replacing an HVAC system is the size and type of your system. HVAC systems come in different sizes, measured in tons or BTUs (British Thermal Units), which indicate how much heat they can remove or add to your home per hour.

The size of your system depends on the square footage and cooling or heating load of your home, which are influenced by factors such as insulation, windows, orientation, climate, and personal preferences. A professional HVAC contractor can perform a load calculation to determine the optimal size for your home.

The type of your system also affects the cost. There are different types of HVAC systems, such as central air conditioners, heat pumps, furnaces, boilers, ductless mini-splits, geothermal heat pumps, and more. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages, depending on your home’s design, climate, and energy efficiency goals.

According to HomeAdvisor1, the average cost to replace an HVAC system by unit type is:

  • The size and capacity of the unit. The bigger and more powerful the unit, the more it will cost to buy and install. You need to choose a unit that matches your home’s cooling and heating needs, as well as your budget.
  • The efficiency and quality of the unit. The higher the efficiency rating (SEER) of the unit, the more energy-efficient it will be and the more money you will save on your utility bills. However, higher-efficiency units also tend to be more expensive upfront. You need to balance the initial cost with the long-term savings when choosing a unit.

The complexity and location of the installation. The more difficult and time-consuming the installation is, the more it will cost in labor and materials. Some factors that can affect the installation cost are:

  • Central air conditioner: $2,500 to $7,500
  • Heat pump: $3,800 to $8,200
  • Furnace: $2,000 to $5,400
  • Boiler: $2,200 to $10,000
  • Ductless mini-split: $3,000 to $10,000
  • Geothermal heat pump: $15,000 to $35,000

Factor #2: The Size and Layout of Your Home

The second factor that affects the cost of replacing an HVAC system is the size and layout of your home. The larger your home is, the more cooling or heating capacity you will need from your system. The layout of your home also affects how easy or difficult it is to install ductwork and wiring for your system.

According to HomeAdvisor, the average cost to replace an HVAC system by home size is:

  • 1,200 sq. ft.: $2,215
  • 1,500 sq. ft.: $3,435
  • 2,000 sq. ft.: $3,402
  • 2,500 sq. ft.: $3,945
  • 3,000 sq. ft.: $4,685
  • 4,000 sq. ft.: $5,215

These costs do not include installation labor fees.

Factor #3: The Condition and Length of Your Ductwork

The third factor that affects the cost of replacing an HVAC system is the condition and length of your ductwork. Ductwork is the network of pipes that carries conditioned air throughout your home. If you have existing ductwork that is in good shape and compatible with your new system, you can save money by reusing it.

However, if your ductwork is old, damaged, leaky, poorly insulated or sealed or not sized properly for your new system you may need to repair or replace it entirely. This can add significantly to the cost of your project.

According to HomeGuide2, the average cost to install ductwork for an HVAC system is:

  • Ductwork installation: $1.50 to $4 per linear foot
  • Ductwork materials: $0.50 to $2 per linear foot
  • Ductwork labor: $45 to $75 per hour

The total cost depends on the length and complexity of your ductwork layout.

Factor #4: The Brand and Efficiency Rating of Your New Unit

The fourth factor that affects the cost of replacing an HVAC system is the brand and efficiency rating of your new unit. There are many brands of HVAC systems on the market, each with its own reputation, quality, features, and warranty. Some of the popular brands are:

  • Carrier
  • Trane
  • Lennox
  • Rheem
  • Goodman
  • York
  • American Standard

The brand you choose may affect the cost of your system, as well as its performance, reliability, and customer service. You should compare different brands and models to find the one that suits your needs and budget.

The efficiency rating of your new unit also affects the cost. The efficiency rating measures how well your system converts energy into cooling or heating output. The higher the efficiency rating, the less energy your system consumes and the lower your utility bills.

The efficiency rating of HVAC systems is expressed in SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) for cooling and AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency) for heating. The minimum SEER rating for central air conditioners is 13, while the minimum AFUE rating for furnaces is 80%. However, you can find units with higher ratings that can save you more money in the long run.

According to HomeGuide, the average cost to replace an HVAC system by efficiency rating is:

  • 13 SEER / 80% AFUE: $4,000 to $6,000
  • 14 SEER / 90% AFUE: $5,000 to $7,000
  • 16 SEER / 95% AFUE: $6,000 to $8,000
  • 18 SEER / 98% AFUE: $7,000 to $10,000

These costs do not include installation labor fees.

Factor #5: The Installation Labor Fees

The fifth factor that affects the cost of replacing an HVAC system is the installation labor fees. Installing an HVAC system is a complex and technical job that requires a licensed and experienced contractor. The labor fees depend on the contractor’s hourly rate, the scope and difficulty of the work, and the location and season of the project.

According to HomeAdvisor, the average installation labor fees for an HVAC system are:

  • Installation labor: $500 to $2,500
  • Removal and disposal of old unit: $300 to $500
  • Permits and inspections: $100 to $300

The total cost depends on the contractor’s quote and the local regulations.

Factor #6: The Upgrades and Add-ons

The sixth factor that affects the cost of replacing an HVAC system is the upgrades and add-ons. Depending on your home’s needs and preferences, you may want to add some features or accessories to your new system to enhance its performance, comfort, or efficiency. Some of the common upgrades and add-ons are:

  • Thermostat: $50 to $300
  • Air purifier: $600 to $3,000
  • Humidifier or dehumidifier: $300 to $2,500
  • Zoning system: $2,000 to $3,500
  • Variable-speed blower: $600 to $1,200
  • Smart vents: $100 to $200 each

The total cost depends on the type and number of upgrades and add-ons you choose.
